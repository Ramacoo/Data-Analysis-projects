## Projects

### 1. Loan Default Analysis 🏦
[![Open In Colab](https://colab.research.google.com/assets/colab-badge.svg)](https://colab.research.google.com/drive/1Y2Cd4vxT39UCjPrrpUWtsWUJXew1sAZT?usp=sharing)

**Tools:** Python, pandas, matplotlib, seaborn

**Question:** Do age, gender, income, and employment status affect loan repayment behavior?

**Key Findings:**
- Gender and age alone had little bearing on loan default
- Small business owners had the highest default rate (41.4%) — likely due to exposure to economic shocks like inflation and currency depreciation in the Ghanaian market
- Big business owners had the lowest default rate (31.6%)
- The loan-to-income ratio proved more revealing than income alone
- Unemployed people who fully repaid had the highest loan-to-income ratio (5.19) — revealing that "unemployed" in Ghana captures informal workers, students, and others whose repayment behaviors vary significantly

### 2. Student Scholarship Categorization 🎓
[![Open In Colab](https://colab.research.google.com/assets/colab-badge.svg)](https://colab.research.google.com/drive/1hEmiquhQRfW5T9v1i6CbbufAHWL3scyw?usp=sharing)

**Tools:** Python, pandas, matplotlib, seaborn

**Dataset:** Students Performance in Exams (Kaggle)

**Question:** How can we fairly identify students who qualify for full vs partial scholarships considering both academic performance and socioeconomic background?

**Key Findings:**
- Designed a needs-aware merit scholarship system with two tracks — merit based and equity based
- Students facing both free/reduced lunch AND low parental education scored up to 62 points lower on average — confirming compounding disadvantage
- Partial scholarship recipients scored significantly higher (275-300) than full scholarship recipients (200-266) — intentional by design to prioritize disadvantaged students
